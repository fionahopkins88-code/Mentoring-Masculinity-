Frequently Asked Questions

Everything You Need to Know

Common questions from parents about MM, the founding cohort, and how mentoring with Cal works.

What age range is this for?

MM works with young men aged 10 to 18. The mentoring approach adapts to each individual's maturity and stage of development — a 10-year-old and a 17-year-old need very different things, and Cal adjusts accordingly.

Is this one-to-one or group?

The founding cohort is a small, close group with significant individual attention from Cal. Each young man also receives personalised guidance based on his specific needs and goals. It's the best of both — group accountability with individual focus.

How often are sessions?

Sessions run weekly. Consistency is the foundation of everything we build — showing up every week is non-negotiable. Real change doesn't come from one-off sessions.

Can parents speak to Cal first?

Absolutely — and it's encouraged. Every enrolment starts with a parent consultation, either in person or online. You'll meet Cal before your son does. Your trust matters, and Cal earns it face-to-face.

Is there WhatsApp support between sessions?

Yes. Founding cohort members have direct WhatsApp access to Cal for check-ins, accountability, and support between sessions. Your son isn't on his own between meetings.

What's included in the founding cohort?

Weekly structured sessions, direct WhatsApp access to Cal, a personal accountability system, regular progress check-ins with parents, first access to future programmes, and founding member pricing locked in permanently.

How much does it cost?

Founding cohort pricing is introductory and will increase for all future cohorts. Exact pricing is shared during the parent consultation so Cal can explain the full value of what's included and answer any questions.

Where are sessions held?

Sessions are held in person. Specific location details are shared during the consultation process.

Is this therapy?

No. Cal is a mentor, not a therapist. If he believes your son needs clinical support, he'll tell you honestly and help you find the right person. MM is about building structure, accountability, and confidence — not clinical treatment.

What if my son doesn't want to do it?

That's normal — and it's fine. Cal is used to meeting resistance with patience. Most young men come around once they realise he's not another adult telling them what to do. He's the person in their corner.
